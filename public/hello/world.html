Market Price Evaluation & P2P Trading in Vaidyuti Energy Exchange Network

by Rithvik Nishad & Muhammed Ashiq Abdul Khader

Terminologies and Context

  • Prosumer A prosumer is a producer or consumer of energy participating in the Vaidyuti Energy Exchange Network. It can be a house/office/factory (any individually addressable and billable entity) installed with or without Solar Rooftop, battery storage or any other source of energy generation. Prosumers maybe an External Prosumer based on their type of participation/interaction with the network. Some entities that are virtually modeled or represented as a prosumer in the network that may not be fully compliant with Vaidyuti are classified as external prosumer. This includes any external source/sink such as another micro-grid (sibling grid) or central grid (parent grid) that may not be capable of flexible buying/selling price.
  • Vaidyuti Network and Network Class Vaidyuti Network is an addressable group or a collection of prosumers connected together to form a network. Networks are classified as Class A/B/C/D/E. A Class A network is the highest level of network in the context of Vaidyuti. Bridge connections can only be used to interconnect two or more Class A networks with each other to exchange energy (i.e., Class A networks only have sibling networks and not a parent network). Class B networks are prosumers of a Class A network. Similarly for Class C and D. Class D network is the lowest network where it’s prosumers are the individually addressable and billable entities and is at the edge of the network. The edge prosumers of the Class D network are of Class E network type, but it is a virtual network defined by the Vaidyuti Compliant meter to allow differential pricing when energy export can be from multiple types of sources (example: battery, solar, wind etc.) owned by the prosumers of Class D network.
    • Sibling Network Is a Vaidyuti Network that is of the same class as that of the reference network. For example if the reference network is of Class D, then the sibling network is also of Class D. Sibling networks can be an external prosumer if that network is not Vaidyuti compliant.
    • Parent Network Is a Vaidyuti Network that is of a higher class when compared to the class level of the reference network. For example if the reference network is of Class D, then the parent network is of Class C. Class B network is not a parent network of a Class D network, instead they are called Ancestor Network.
  • Internal Generation Is the sum of all generation of non-external prosumers that are producing energy at the time of reference.
  • External Generation Is the sum of all generation of external prosumers that are producing energy at the time of reference.

Category Specific Selling Price

Evaluated only for prosumers of Class D Networks, since energy source of prosumers are of definite type only at the edge of the network. Higher classes would have energy mix from various types.

For a particular network, base price for a each type of energy source is defined based on various factors such as market value, expected ROI for that type of source, etc. For example, base price for Solar energy source may be set to INR 4.67, for wind may be set to INR 5.34, for Li-Ion battery packs may be set to INR 6.29, and so on.

Category Specific Market Price considers factors such as how much energy a type of source is expected to yield in certain amount of years, the ROI yielded in that amount of years, and so on. This promotes competitive pricing such that prosumers are encouraged to use the most efficient type of energy source, hence yields good profit margin when compared to the category market price. If a prosumer is able to generate solar energy for INR 3.00 and the Solar Market Price is INR 4.67, the prosumer has a profit of INR 1.67 for every unit of energy sold.

Only the middleware servers must be authorized to write to specific MQTT topics / endpoints that are related to the authorized selling and buying price of individual prosumers. Similarly, only the prosumer shall be authorized to update its net power export/import related topics.

Peer-to-peer trading in Vaidyuti Energy Exchange Network

Vaidyuti DEX (Decentralized Energy Exchange) is a generic peer-to-peer trading platform. This platform is used to register and authorize trades between prosumers in the network. Sellers can post information related to the energy that is exported to the grid and buyers can view available sellers that suit their needs and buy energy through the DEX platform. However, the DEX trading platform is not directly exposed to the prosumers as they may not make the most efficient trades with other peer in real-time. Hence another automated trading layer is required in the Vaidyuti stack.

VEX (Vaidyuti Energy Exchange) Platform

VEX is the platform where prosumers can just broadcast their meter readings and VEX creates most efficient and cost effective trade between peers. VEX uses DEX as the underlying platform for peer-to-peer energy exchange trades. Vaidyuti stack contains many services such as Telegraf for data aggregation from the prosumers, InfluxDB to store and query time-series data in the database, MQTT as the primary messaging and communication protocol and so on. Vaidyuti compliant smart meter just broadcasts their net import or export power along with the source/sink type, and VEX will periodically query these time-series information and create most efficient trades by analyzing every peers in the network.

How VEX automates trades between peers?

VEX converts import/export power time-series data into discrete chunks of energy packets such that the power variance is within a certain limit and it does not crosses zero (i.e., a chunk is either export or import only). After identifying the discrete chunks, each buy chunk/operation is matched with a sell chunk/operation by highest score method until it satisfies it’s demand. The score of a trade is evaluated based on the distance between the peers (that is responsible for the losses/efficiency of transmission) and the selling price set by selling peer.
